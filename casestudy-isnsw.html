The challenge

A 60-year-old institution needed to speak to today.

AISNSW had proudly served the NSW independent schools sector for nearly six decades. But its digital presence and brand identity had not kept pace with the sector's growth and ambition. With one in five NSW children now attending an independent school, the organisation needed a brand that reflected its influence and vision.

The project was a full rebrand - new name, new visual identity, new website - and I was embedded in the team responsible for translating that into a seamless digital experience. Every decision had to balance the gravitas of a peak body with the accessibility needs of members across the state.

Design process

Structured thinking, iterative making.

Here's how I approached the work — from diagnosis through to a polished, tested design system ready for development handoff.

01
Discover
Stakeholder interviews & site audit
Ran in-depth interviews with 12 stakeholders across schools and the ISNSW team. Audited the existing site for IA, content gaps, and accessibility issues. Benchmarked against comparable peak bodies nationally.
FigJam Hotjar Screaming Frog
02
Define
IA restructure & user flows
Rebuilt the information architecture from scratch based on card sorting sessions with real users. Mapped out 8 key user journeys and identified critical moments of friction to resolve.
Miro Optimal Workshop
03
Design
Wireframes to high-fidelity UI
Designed lo-fi wireframes for all key templates, iterated through two rounds of usability testing with Maze before moving to high-fidelity. Built a comprehensive design system with 40+ components.
Figma Maze
04
Deliver
Dev handoff & QA
Prepared detailed design specs, component documentation, and motion guidelines. Worked closely alongside the development team throughout build, conducting structured QA against the Figma source of truth.
Figma Dev Mode Zeplin

What I learned.
